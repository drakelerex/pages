The European Garden Spider

Names and Range of the European Garden Spider

The European Garden Spider is an Orbweaver commonly called diadem spider, orangie, cross spider and crowned orb weaver. It's scientific name is Araneus diadematus. They are found in Europe, across North America, and from British Columbia to Newfoundland. These spiders were introduced to North America by Western and Northern Europe.

Sizes and markings

Colors can range from extremely light yellow to very dark grey, but all spiders have mottled white markings across the dorsal abdomen, forming a cross.
There is an atyplical variation that doesn't have the cross
Adult female spiders range in length from 6.5 to 20 mm, and males range in length from 5.5 to 13 mm.

Behavior

The legs of these spiders are specialized for making orb webs. The female makes the web and hangs in the center of the web or in nearby foliage. When prey lands on the web, the spider feels a disturbance and bites the prey and wraps it in silk, saving it for later. These spiders rarely bite humans.
Some Orb Web spiders, because the silk is metaboliclly costly, recycle their webs routinly in the morning and evening or in anticipation of a storm. Their diet is flying insects such as flies. A bee sting is more painful than a European Garden Spider bite. If you disturb a Garden Spider it will use it legs to oscillate itself up and down and shake the web, if it is severely disturbed it will leave the web until it's convinced the danger has passed.
